I am writing to apply for this planning role. I hold a Master of Architecture from the University of Technology Sydney and I am looking to move into development assessment or strategic planning, with a genuine interest in building a long-term career in local government.

My graduating studio focused on housing in Sydney, drawing on government proposals around transport-oriented development and the NSW Housing Pattern Book. Working across multiple scales pushed me to think about architecture not just as individual buildings, but as part of a broader system shaped by policy, urban conditions, and community need. That experience gave me a real appreciation for the planning frameworks that underpin good built environment outcomes.

Over the course of my degree I built working knowledge of LEP and DCP controls, completed coursework in Urban Analytics and Digital Representation using QGIS, and carried out extensive site and urban context analysis across Sydney-based projects. I have been actively familiarising myself with the Environmental Planning and Assessment Act 1979 and the broader NSW planning system as I work toward making this transition.

The role involves applying council policies to resolve operational and customer matters, preparing compliance and incident reports, managing enquiries from the public, and working closely with staff, contractors and community stakeholders. It is not a planning role, but it has given me direct exposure to the governance structures, accountability requirements and community expectations that planning sits within.

I believe the combination of my architectural training and my experience inside a council puts me in a good position to contribute meaningfully from early on. I am someone who takes their work seriously, communicates well, and is genuinely motivated by the idea of a career in planning.
